VeinTrack Beta

Private vein-health tracking for everyday context.

VeinTrack helps people track vein-health-related daily context on device, including symptoms, leg elevation, compression use, activity and rest sessions, weight, sleep, travel context, consultations, photos, and reports.

VeinTrack supports personal tracking, reminders, and report preparation. It does not diagnose, treat, or replace professional medical advice.

Private by design

The dashboard keeps context on your device.

The home screen brings today's cards together without requiring a Duck Code account: weight, activity and rest, travel mode, care events, guides, and report entry points stay close at hand.

  • Daily cards are stored locally unless you export or share them.
  • Apple Health and Motion access only after permission is granted.
  • No Duck Code backend account or sale of personal data.

Quick View

Save the essentials on busy days.

Quick View turns the repeated bits of a day into a quick pass: activity, sleep, leg elevation, pain, swelling, and photos can be captured without opening several separate forms.

Those entries still become normal app records, so they can appear later in stats, insights, reports, exports, and calendar summaries.

Activity and rest coaching

Activity and rest live beside the rest of the day.

The dashboard keeps steps, walking distance, longest exercise, rest context, and flight-mode time in the same daily flow as care and compression records.

VeinTrack can use on-device activity context to help identify longer walking or running sessions and remind you to rest. Passive reminders may be delayed by iOS background scheduling. Coached activity sessions are more timely because you intentionally start or accept an active session.

The app frames reminders as comfort and rest prompts, not medical instructions.

Daily events and photos

Turn quick choices into useful event history.

Pain, swelling, leg elevation, photos, and daily notes can be saved together, then reviewed later as part of the calendar, reports, and personal comparisons.

Photos and files remain part of local app data unless you choose to export, share, email, back up, or save them elsewhere.

Calendar and history

Browse records by day instead of memory.

Calendar markers make small daily entries easier to find again. The month view helps you review what happened, when it happened, and which notes or attachments belong with each record.

Stats, insights, and reports

See patterns without pretending they are answers.

Insights and charts help summarize what you recorded over time, including pain, swelling, activity, rest, photos, consultation records, and other context.

VeinTrack can generate reports from tracked data, including summaries, charts, photo comparisons, and appendices with raw data.

You decide when to export or share them.

Widgets and Live Activities

Keep reminders visible when you choose.

Settings collect reminder timing, activity and health permissions, preferences, and related controls in one place. VeinTrack may use notifications, widgets, and Live Activities to show reminders and activity or rest session status.

These features are controlled by iOS permissions and user settings.

Export and share

Share summaries only when you choose.

The Stats Summary view gives you a compact place to review all-time counts, recent context, and report actions before creating a PDF, HTML email summary, CSV export, or backup.

Generated reports, files, and backups are only shared when you choose to export, email, save, or send them elsewhere.

Privacy

No backend account, no sale of user data.

Depending on configuration, VeinTrack may handle user-entered health context, pain and swelling scores, rest records, compression details, activity session records, Apple Health sleep and weight data when permission is granted, Motion activity data when permission is granted, user-attached photos, consultation details, reports, exports, backups, and optional StoreKit support purchase status.
